This isomorphism, and its inverse, are particular cases of a common construction (Bourbaki's "Algbre IX", 9, no. 2-3; Ricardo Baeza's "Quadratic Forms over Semilocal Rings", Lecture Notes in Mathematics 655, Springer 1978): If f and g are two bilinear forms on a module V, and if F and G are their corresponding quadratic forms (so F(x) = f(x, x) and G(x) = g(x, x)), then there is a canonical isomorphism from Cl(V, F) to Cl(V, F + G), which however depends on g and not just on G.

- Use this Clifford-exterior isomorphism to define constant coefficients and scalar products on Clifford algebras. This will depend on a bilinear form.

- Check if the scalar product on the Clifford algebra allows a faster way to compute the lifted bilinear form on the exterior algebra.
